## §2 — What the data says (full sweep)

`sp001_full_sweep_mi300x_ws8.csv` covers `collectives = {all_reduce, all_gather, reduce_scatter, all_to_all}`
× `dtypes = {fp16, bf16}` at `world_size = 8` over `total_bytes ∈ [1 KiB .. 1 GiB]` (powers of two,
clipped per-collective to keep the symmetric heap below 8 GiB). 148 paired (iris, rccl) cells total.

| collective       | dtype | n cells | fail / n   | min ratio | max ratio | worst-size |
|------------------|-------|--------:|-----------:|----------:|----------:|------------|
| all_reduce       | fp16  |      21 | **21/21**  |    1.94×  |    3.35×  | 1 KiB      |
| all_reduce       | bf16  |      21 | **21/21**  |    1.13×  |    4.86×  | 4 KiB      |
| all_gather       | fp16  |      18 | **14/18**  |    0.86×  |    1.82×  | 512 KiB    |
| all_gather       | bf16  |      18 | **14/18**  |    0.85×  |    2.18×  | 64 KiB     |
| reduce_scatter   | fp16  |      14 | **14/14**  |    1.19×  |    1.86×  | 128 KiB    |
| reduce_scatter   | bf16  |      14 | **14/14**  |    1.19×  |    1.69×  | 1 GiB      |
| all_to_all       | fp16  |      21 | **21/21**  |    1.37×  |    3.23×  | 4 KiB      |
| all_to_all       | bf16  |      21 | **19/21**  |    0.38×  |    2.90×  | 1 MiB      |

(Per-cell rows in `sp001_full_sweep_mi300x_ws8.csv`. 8-panel plot in
`sp001_full_sweep_mi300x_ws8.png`. The pivoted JSON summary is regenerated
by passing `--output_summary` to the reproduction command in §4 — the
`*.json` glob in `.gitignore` keeps it out of the tree.)

**Headline numbers**:

- **138 / 148 cells (93 %)** exceed the 10 % RCCL acceptance gate.
- All 8 (collective × dtype) cells contain at least one over-gate size; **6 of 8** cells fail at 100 %.
- Worst observed slowdown: **4.86×** on `all_reduce/bf16` at 4 KiB.
- iris is faster than RCCL only in the small-message tail of `all_gather` and `all_to_all` (10 / 148 sub-1.10× cells).

The defaults-table plumbing is end-to-end correct and the harness works. The acceptance
gate is unmet because the underlying kernels cannot match RCCL throughput today — not
because of a routing/Config bug. No table-tuning revision can close a 4.86× kernel gap.

## §3 — Why the perf gate cannot be closed in this sprint

Closing C4 requires kernel-level work — a new low-latency path for ≤ 64 KiB
`all_reduce`, a different reduce-scatter scheduling strategy, and re-tiling
work for `all_to_all`. That is multiple weeks of GPU-bound engineering, not a
"minimal, surgical" revision-pass scope.

The defaults-table abstraction (`(arch, collective, msg_size_bucket) → kernel knobs`)
keyed by the harness output is the right structure for that work to plug into:
once a faster kernel exists, only the corresponding bucket entries in
`_DEFAULTS_TABLE` need to flip — no re-plumbing of any collective is required.
The branch as-shipped therefore delivers the correct **plumbing and harness scope**
of SP-001 (C1, C2, C3, C6) and quantifies the perf gap (C4) end-to-end, leaving
the kernel-parity work to a follow-up sprint.

See `output/revision-notes.md` (in workspace `K-7363`) for the formal escalation
request to split off SP-002 ("iris.ccl kernel parity with RCCL on MI300X").

## §4 — How to reproduce

```bash
# On an MI300X 8-rank node, inside an MC2-pinned rocm/pytorch container:
cd /path/to/iris
pip install -e .
torchrun --nproc_per_node=8 benchmark/ccl/comprehensive_sweep.py \
    --mode validate --benchmark_iris --benchmark_rccl \
    --collectives all_reduce,all_gather,reduce_scatter,all_to_all \
    --dtypes fp16,bf16 \
    --n_warmup 5 --n_repeat 10 \
    --output_csv      benchmark/ccl/sprint_evidence/sp001/sp001_full_sweep_mi300x_ws8.csv \
    --output_summary  /tmp/sp001_full_sweep_mi300x_ws8.summary.json   # gitignored

python3 benchmark/ccl/sprint_evidence/sp001/plot_sp001_full_sweep.py \
    benchmark/ccl/sprint_evidence/sp001/sp001_full_sweep_mi300x_ws8.csv \
    benchmark/ccl/sprint_evidence/sp001/sp001_full_sweep_mi300x_ws8.png
```

The single-cell smoke run from earlier rounds (`sp001_smoke_all_reduce_fp16_ws8_mi300x.csv`
+ `.png`) is preserved on-branch because it carries the per-row `correct` /
`max_abs_err` columns the harness only emits in correctness mode (the full sweep
above runs in latency-only mode so all 148 cells finish in ~22 s).
